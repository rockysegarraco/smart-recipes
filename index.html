Eggnog French Toast

Add to Cart
Ingredients
  • 1 ½ cups eggnog
  • 2 eggs, beaten slightly
  • 1 ½ tablespoons ground cinnamon, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 12 slices French bread
  • Maple syrup (to serve)
Directions
  • Prep: 15 mins
  • Cook: 10 mins
  • Total: 25 mins
  • Servings: 6
  1. Whisk eggnog, eggs, cinnamon, and pumpkin pie spice together in mixing bowl until well blended; pour into shallow dish.
  2. Preheat pan over medium heat or electric skillet to 300 degrees Fahrenheit. Lightly grease skillet.
  3. Dip one slice of bread at a time into eggnog mixture, coating each side of bread thoroughly.
  4. Place prepared bread slices into preheated skillet and cook, turning once, until golden brown on each side.
  5. Place cooked bread slices on serving plate and cover with foil to keep warm until all slices are cooked. Serve immediately.

Cinnamon Apple-tini

Add to Cart
Ingredients Instructions
  • 1/2 c. apple cider
  • 1/4 c. vodka
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp. caramel sauce

Wet edge of 2 martini glasses with apple cider, then dip in cinnamon-sugar mixture. In cocktail shaker filled with ice, combine 1/2 cup apple cider, 1/4 cup vodka, 1 teaspoon cinnamon, and 1 tablespoon caramel sauce. Pour into glasses.

A Cheesy Delight: Fried Ravioli

Add to Cart
Ingredients Instructions
  • ½ cup flour
  • 2 eggs (beaten)
  • 1 cup Italian-style breadcrumbs
  • 1 package ravioli (9 ounces)
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
Instructions
  1. Add each of the first three ingredients to a separate bowl.
  2. Dip ravioli in flour, then eggs, then breadcrumbs.
  3. In batches, lay ravioli in single layer in basket; air-fry at 350 degrees Fahrenheit for 8 minutes per batch, flipping halfway through. Serve with marinara sauce.

Mashed Pumpkin

Ingredients
  • 2.6 lbs. piece pumpkin, peeled, seeded, chopped coarsely
  • 1.5 oz. butter, chopped
  • 1 Tbsp. finely chopped fresh chives
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • whole nutmeg
Instructions
  1. Steam or microwave the pumpkin until tender; drain well.
  2. Mash the pumpkin and butter in a large bowl until smooth. Stir in chopped chives; season to taste with salt and pepper.
  3. Grate a little nutmeg over mashed pumpkin.

Ravioli Lasagna

Add to Cart
Ingredients
  • 1 Tbs. + 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 lb. mild or spicy Italian sausage (casing removed)
  • 4½ cups marinara sauce
  • 2 lbs. frozen cheese ravioli, thawed (about 24 ravioli)
  • 2 ½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup freshly grated parmesan cheese
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven 400°F.
  2. Brush 9-by-13-inch pan with 1 tsp. olive oil. Heat large skillet on medium heat. Add remaining tablespoon olive oil and sausage. Cook, breaking sausage into bite-sized pieces with the back of a wooden spoon, until sausage begins to brown; about 8 minutes. Add marinara and simmer. Remove from heat and cool slightly.
  3. Spoon a third of the sauce mixture over bottom of pan. Place layer of ravioli on top of sauce. Pour half of remaining sauce on top of ravioli. Sprinkle sauce with one cup mozzarella and ½ cup parmesan. Repeat with another layer of ravioli. Top with remaining sauce, mozzarella, and parmesan.
  4. Bake uncovered 25 minutes or until edges are bubbling. Switch oven to broil and broil on high 3 to 4 minutes or until cheese is golden brown. Allow lasagna to sit 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!

Manuka Honey Peanut Butter Blossoms

Add to Cart
Ingredients
  • 1 stick plant butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup creamy natural peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Comvita Manuka Honey
  • egg replacer equivalent of 1 egg, such as Bob’s Red Mill (Buy from Amazon, $4.39)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar, for rolling
  • 15 kisses or large vegan chocolate chips
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Using an electric hand mixer, cream the butter in a medium bowl. Add in the peanut butter, brown sugar, manuka honey, egg replacer, and vanilla extract, using the mixer.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flours, baking soda, and salt. Add the wet ingredients to the dry and combine.
  4. Gather the dough, form it into a ball, wrap it in plastic, and refrigerate for 20-30 minutes, until firm enough to roll into balls.
  5. Place the 1/2 cup of sugar into a small bowl. Use a small ice cream scoop to scoop about 1 tablespoon of the dough. Form each scoop into a ball and roll in the sugar. Place on a Silpat or parchment-lined baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 9 to 10 minutes, until cracked on top. Remove from the oven and press kisses or chips into the middle of the cookie.
